CANADA COUNTRY

characteristics

In 1867, Canada became a confederation of four provinces: Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick, and Nova Scotia.

Canadian Pacific Railway was completed in 1885. It was a major engineering feat that connected the eastern and western parts of Canada.

The Klondike Gold Rush took place in the late 1890s, attracting thousands of prospectors to the Yukon in search of gold.

In 1917, the Halifax Explosion occurred when a French cargo ship carrying explosives collided with another vessel in the Halifax Harbor, causing a massive explosion and widespread destruction.

In 1967, Canada celebrated its centennial, marking 100 years since confederation. Expo 67, a world's fair held in Montreal, showcased Canada's achievements and cultural diversity.

In 1972, Canada implemented a new national policy called "Official Bilingualism," recognizing English and French as the official languages of the country.

The Canadian Constitution was patriated in 1982, giving Canada full control over its constitution and adding the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.

In 1999, the territory of Nunavut was established, becoming Canada's newest and largest territory. It is predominantly inhabited by Inuit people.

The Winter Olympics were held twice in Canada in the past. Calgary hosted the games in 1988, and Vancouver hosted in 2010.

Famous cultural symbols

-The most famous cultural symbol of Canada was undoubtedly the maple leaf, the red leaf that illustrated the country's flag.

-In fact, it was from the maple tree that the traditional maple syrup was extracted, that delicious syrup similar to honey that inundated American pancakes at breakfast.

-distinctive element was the beaver, called the beaver in Canada, and considered the country's animal symbol, which adorned coins, coats of arms, and official postage stamps.

Celebrations and typical dates

like Christmas, Halloween, and Thanksgiving were taken very seriously in Canada, with long preparations and preservation of the traditions and symbols of each event.

Christmas (December 25th), Canadians used to set up trees with real pine trees, ensuring they were reused after the holiday.

They also dedicated themselves to illuminating the entire city and wore Christmas-themed clothing in the streets. They prepared delicious typical foods such as butter tarts (a pie made with sugar, eggs, butter, and maple syrup).

In the autumn, Canadians already began preparations for Halloween, including festive visits to pumpkin farms.

On Halloween (October 31st), children perpetuated the tradition of "trick or treating," and everyone dressed up as witches, ghosts, and monsters.

The zombie walk was also a classic in Canadian lands, gathering people dressed up as the undead walking through the streets.

Canada Day (July 1st) was marked by thousands of people celebrating the country's unification in public events organized by the government, which included concerts, lunches, parades, contests, and fireworks displays.

There was also Remembrance Day (November 11th), when the memory of young people who died during the two world wars was honored with two minutes of silence.

Typical Canadian Foods

Former Canadian Prime Minister Joe Clark defined Canadian cuisine as "a cuisine of many cuisines" since the country has an immense variety of typical dishes with diverse cultural influences.

However, considering that Canadian roots lie in French, English, and Indigenous culture, it is from this blend that its main flavors emerge.

Let's get to know some classic recipes:

Poutine

Poutine is the most traditional dish in Canada, created in Quebec and consumed throughout the country.

The recipe consists of basically french fries topped with cheddar cheese curds and covered in special meat gravy.

Beaver Tail (Rabo de Castor)

The famous "beaver tail" is a highly caloric treat made with fried dough and topped with Nutella, chocolate, or peanut butter.

The name comes from its resemblance to the rodent's tail, and the sweet treat can be topped with any desired topping.

Peameal Bacon

Peameal bacon is a delicacy consisting of bacon made from pork loin and coated in cornmeal.

It is usually served in sandwiches and can be purchased at butchers and markets.

Top Canadian Sights

Falls:strong Niagara Falls attracted millions of visitors each year.

National Park: Banff National Park welcomed numerous tourists who came to explore its stunning landscapes and wildlife.

Vancouver: Vancouver was a popular destination for tourists due to its vibrant city life and proximity to natural attractions like Stanley Park and Whistler.

Toronto: Toronto received a significant number of visitors who were drawn to its iconic landmarks such as the CN Tower, Royal Ontario Museum, and vibrant multicultural atmosphere.

Quebec City: Quebec City's historic charm and European ambiance attracted many tourists who wanted to experience its rich history and visit landmarks like the Château Frontenac and Old Quebec.

Montreal: Montreal was a favorite destination for tourists interested in its dynamic arts and culture scene, festivals like the Montreal Jazz Festival, and exploring historic neighborhoods like Old Montreal.

Ottawa: Ottawa was a popular choice for tourists, especially during the summer months, to visit attractions like Parliament Hill, Rideau Canal, and various museums and galleries.

Whistler: Whistler, known for its world-class ski resorts, drew numerous visitors seeking winter sports and outdoor recreational activities.

Victoria: Victoria, the capital of British Columbia, was visited by tourists who wanted to explore the beautiful Butchart Gardens, the historic Inner Harbour, and take part in whale watching tours.

Halifax:Halifax attracted tourists who were interested in its maritime heritage, historical sites like the Citadel, and exploring the scenic coastline of Nova Scotia.
